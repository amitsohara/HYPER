# Principle

HyperMind does not reason directly over raw inputs, isolated memories, or language alone.

Instead, HyperMind constructs, maintains, and continuously evolves an internal World Model that represents its current understanding of reality.

All higher cognitive processes operate over this evolving World Model.

# Cognitive Laws

## Law 1 — World Before Reasoning

No specialist may perform reasoning directly on raw input if a corresponding world representation can be constructed.

---

## Law 2 — Shared Reality

Every specialist operates on the same canonical World Model.

No specialist maintains a private, conflicting representation unless explicitly authorized for simulation or experimentation.

---

## Law 3 — Dynamic Reality

The World Model is continuously updated.

Reality is never considered static.

Every observation may refine the current understanding.

---

## Law 4 — Multi-Perspective Reality

The World Model may contain multiple competing hypotheses simultaneously.

Contradictory information is represented explicitly until sufficient evidence resolves uncertainty.

---

## Law 5 — Temporal Reality

Every entity and relationship has temporal validity.

The World Model distinguishes:

Past

Present

Predicted Future

Hypothetical Futures

---

## Law 6 — Causal Reality

Events are connected through explicit causal relationships.

HyperMind seeks to understand why events occur rather than merely recording that they occurred.

---

## Law 7 — Goal-Centered Reality

Goals are represented as first-class components of the World Model.

Reasoning and planning must account for active goals, constraints, and priorities.

---

## Law 8 — Explainable Reality

Every decision must be reconstructable through the sequence of World Model updates that produced it.

---

## Law 9 — Persistent Reality

The World Model persists across cognitive sessions.

HyperMind evolves its understanding of reality over time.

---

## Law 10 — Reality Before Language

Natural language is treated as an interface for describing reality, not as reality itself.

The internal representation remains structured, persistent, and language-independent.

# Engineering Rules

Every future HyperMind subsystem shall:

Read from the World Model.

Contribute updates to the World Model.

Communicate updates exclusively through HCNS.

Coordinate through HCSE.

Maintain full traceability through HRDD.

No subsystem may bypass the World Model unless explicitly justified by an approved architectural decision.

---

# Success Criteria

The World-Centric Cognition Principle is considered successfully implemented when:

A persistent canonical World Model exists.

All cognitive specialists operate on the shared World Model.

Reasoning is performed over structured world representations rather than raw language.

World updates are observable, replayable, and explainable.

Temporal, causal, and uncertainty information are preserved.

The World Model continuously evolves through observation, interaction, learning, and verification.

---

# Constitutional Statement

HyperMind understands the world by constructing an evolving internal representation of reality.

Every observation enriches this representation.

Every decision emerges from it.

Every learning experience refines it.

The World Model is therefore the cognitive foundation upon which all higher intelligence within HyperMind is built.
