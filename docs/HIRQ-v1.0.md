## Purpose
The HyperMind Intelligence Research Questions (HIRQ) define the fundamental unanswered scientific questions that must be addressed to build a computational theory of general intelligence.

HIRQ does not assume the answers. It defines the research agenda that guides every future component of HyperMind. Every hypothesis, mathematical model, algorithm, and implementation must trace back to one or more HIRQ questions.

## Vision
To discover, formalize, implement, and experimentally validate the computational principles that give rise to general intelligence.

## Scientific Principles
HyperMind adopts the following scientific principles:
1. Intelligence is investigated through evidence, not assumptions.
2. Every hypothesis must be experimentally testable.
3. Every implementation must be traceable to theory.
4. Every theory may be revised when evidence contradicts it.
5. Scientific honesty takes priority over architectural elegance.
6. Intelligence is measured by capability, adaptability, and learning—not by model size.

## Research Workflow
Every research question follows the same lifecycle:

Research Question → Literature Review → Research Hypothesis → Mathematical Model → Algorithm Design → Implementation → Experiment → Evidence → Revision

## Traceability Framework
Every artifact in HyperMind must be traceable.

HIRQ Question → HCT Hypothesis → HCM Mathematical Model → HCA Algorithm → HMCR Runtime → Experiment → Evidence

No implementation exists without a corresponding research question.

## Success Criteria
HyperMind advances when it demonstrates measurable improvements in:
* Learning speed
* Knowledge transfer
* Multi-step reasoning
* Long-term planning
* Adaptation to new environments
* Decision quality
* Explainability
* Robustness under uncertainty
* Autonomous improvement
* Safe operation

## Closing Statement
HIRQ v1.0 establishes the scientific agenda of the HyperMind Research Program. It does not claim to know the answers to intelligence. Instead, it defines the questions that must be answered through theory, mathematics, engineering, and experimentation. Every future subsystem of HyperMind exists to investigate, implement, and validate these questions.

As Chief Scientist, my recommendation is to freeze HIRQ v1.0 as the constitutional research document for HyperMind.

From here, the next document should be HCT v1.0 (HyperMind Cognitive Theory), where we begin proposing explicit hypotheses for each HIRQ question. Every hypothesis should be labeled as:
* Established Knowledge (supported by existing research),
* Engineering Principle (well-supported design practice), or
* HyperMind Research Hypothesis (our own testable proposal).

That structure will give HyperMind a rigorous scientific foundation while keeping a clear distinction between known science and our original research ideas.
